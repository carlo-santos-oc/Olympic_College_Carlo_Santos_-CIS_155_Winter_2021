Coronavirus

COVID-19 comes from the Corona family of viruses. This category of viruses are common among animals such as camels and bats, but they have been able to infect humans as well. The name Coronavirus refers to the crown-like spikes on the surface of the virus. These spikes allow the virus to embed itself into our cells to take over them and reproduce. Before COVID-19 we have faced other Coronavirus outbreaks in recent history. In 2003 SARS, also known as SARS-COV, first appears in southern China. In 2012 MERS(MERS-COV) appeared in Saudi Arabia and reached surrounding countries as far as South Korea. COVID-19 is also known as SARS-COV2 and shares characteristics from both SARS and MERS.

COVID-19

What we typically refer to as Coronavirus today is COVID-19. It was first detected in 2019 in Wuhan China. This strain is a previously unseen type in humans. Because it has never been seen in humans before we have little to no immunity. COVID-19 also has a Basic Reproduction Number of 2.5. This means one person could potentially infect 2000 people. The symptoms of COVID-19 range from mild to severe and mostly impacts people of advanced age or with previous health issues. While a lot of people may survive with only experiencing mild symptoms, other more vulnerable people ultimately succumb to pneumonia or other complications stemming from the infection.
